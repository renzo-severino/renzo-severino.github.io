Renzo Severino

I am a Ph.D. Student at the Sanford School of Public Policy in Duke University working on Education Economics and Immigration, mainly in Latin America. Before coming to Duke, I majored in Economics from Universidad del Pacífico, Peru. You can find my CV here.

Research

My main research project focuses on the interaction between immigrants and natives in Peruvian classrooms. I make use of large datasets and causal inference methods to characterize school supply dynamics in reaction to the immigration shock, and determine effects on natives' academic performance.

I am also a co-Principal Investigator on a Randomized Control Trial in the Peruvian highlands related to Sexual Education and Native Languages.
