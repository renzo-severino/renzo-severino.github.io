Alternative Approaches to Reducing Teenage Pregnancy

Teenage pregnancy has adverse effects on mother and child health. It also risks teenagersâ€™ educational achievement and economic mobility, especially for vulnerable populations.

In this project, we test two approaches to improving sexual education to prevent unwanted pregnancy among teenagers in Ayacucho, Peru.

  • Mobile Clinic A vehicle staffed by medical proffesionals--who speak the region's native language--goes to each community for a day at a time and delivers on-demand guidance on sex and health issues

  • Teacher Training A four-month teacher training program that emphasizes the importance of teaching sexual education and provides teaching tools to participants

Our sample is 120 schools in around 120 communities in Ayacucho, Peru. We randomized each school into one of three treatment arms:

  1. Clinic + Training: Forty schools receive both the teacher training program and visits from the mobile clinic

  2. Clinic Only: Forty schools receive only visits from the mobile clinic

  3. Control: Forty schools do not receive any intervention
